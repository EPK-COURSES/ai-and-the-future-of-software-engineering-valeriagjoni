# Personal Reflection

During this research, I learned how strongly AI is influencing software testing, debugging, and automation in software engineering. Before studying this topic, I mainly thought of AI as a tool for generating code, but I discovered that AI is also heavily used in testing systems, bug detection, and DevOps automation.

The most surprising thing for me was how AI can automatically generate test cases and analyze software behavior. This can save developers a large amount of time, especially in large projects where manual testing is difficult. I also found it interesting that AI systems can predict which parts of software are most likely to fail after updates.

At the same time, I became more aware of the risks of relying too much on AI. AI-generated code or debugging suggestions may contain mistakes, and developers who blindly trust AI tools may introduce security vulnerabilities or software bugs. I think this is one of the biggest concerns for the future of software engineering.

This research changed my opinion about AI slightly. I now believe that AI will not completely replace software engineers, but it will change their responsibilities. Developers will need to focus more on verification, system understanding, problem solving, and supervision of AI systems rather than only writing code manually.

I also realized that human skills such as critical thinking, communication, and decision-making will remain very important. Even if AI becomes more advanced, software engineers must still understand the systems they build and maintain responsibility for software quality and ethics.

Personally, I plan to adapt by continuously learning new technologies while also strengthening my programming fundamentals. I believe future software engineers must learn how to work together with AI tools instead of competing against them.
